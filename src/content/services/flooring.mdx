faqs:
  - question: "Engineered hardwood, solid hardwood, or LVP — which is right for me?"
    answer: "Engineered hardwood is the GTA default — dimensional stability handles our humidity swings, and it can run over radiant heat. Solid hardwood is best for above-grade with controlled humidity (heritage homes, dry climates). Luxury vinyl plank is the right pick for high-moisture areas, basements, and rental units — modern LVP looks excellent and is nearly indestructible."
  - question: "Can I install new flooring over existing?"
    answer: "Sometimes. Floating LVP and engineered hardwood can sometimes go over existing tile or hardwood if the substrate is flat and the height clearance works at doors. Glue-down and nail-down installations almost always require the existing flooring removed first. We'll check at the consultation."
  - question: "How much does flooring installation cost in the GTA?"

Labour is typically $3–8/sq.ft. depending on the material, subfloor prep, and finishing complexity. We give you a line-item quote at consultation."
  - question: "Do you do stairs?"

  - question: "What about radiant in-floor heat?"
    answer: "We coordinate radiant in-floor heating systems (electric or hydronic) under tile, LVP rated for radiant, or engineered hardwood rated for radiant. This needs to be planned at the design stage — not all materials are compatible."

## Floor matters more than people think

Flooring is the largest visible surface in your home. Get the wrong species, finish, or installation, and the whole space feels off — even if everything else is right. Get it right, and a simple floor swap can transform a room more than a paint job.

## Trade pricing on materials

Our supplier network gives you access to the same hardwood, tile, and stone vendors that price for Toronto's largest residential developers. Walk into a retail showroom and you'll see $14/sq.ft. engineered hardwood; the same product from our supplier comes in at $8–10/sq.ft. We pass that on directly.
