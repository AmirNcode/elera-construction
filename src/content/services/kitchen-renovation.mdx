## Built for Toronto kitchens — house or condo

A kitchen renovation is the most ambitious thing most homeowners do to their home. It touches plumbing, electrical, gas, structural (sometimes), HVAC, cabinetry, stone, tile, drywall, paint, lighting, appliances, and millwork — all coordinated against a cabinetry lead time you can't compress.

We've built these kitchens as new construction for developers across the GTA. The renovation version of that work follows the same playbook: lock the design early, order cabinets first, sequence the trades so each one starts the day after the last one finished, and protect the rest of your home so you can keep living in it.

## Where supplier relationships matter most

Cabinetry, countertops, and tile are the line items where retail markups hurt most. Our supplier accounts — built through years of new-construction work — give you trade pricing on the same finishes the showrooms charge $20–30K more for. That's not a sales line; it's why we don't need to publish package pricing the way retail kitchen companies do.
