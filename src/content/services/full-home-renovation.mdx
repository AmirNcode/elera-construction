## Full-home renovation, run like new construction

A full-home reno is the most complex project a homeowner can undertake. Twenty-plus trades, six-figure budgets, multi-month schedules, and decisions on every surface of your home. Most renovation contractors aren't equipped for it.

Our founder spent 10+ years on the build side of new low-rise residential construction for Toronto's largest developers — projects with the same scale and complexity as a full-home reno, but with the discipline of professional project management. We run renovations with the same playbook: detailed schedules, weekly client meetings, change-order procedures in writing, and milestone-based payment tied to construction verifiables (not the calendar).

## When a full-home reno makes sense vs. piecemeal projects

- **Full reno wins** when you want a coherent design across multiple rooms, when the home needs new mechanicals (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), when walls are coming down, or when you're adding square footage.
- **Piecemeal wins** when only one or two rooms need work and the systems behind the walls are in good shape.

We'll tell you which is the better fit at the consultation — even if the answer is "you don't need a full reno, you need a kitchen and a bathroom."
