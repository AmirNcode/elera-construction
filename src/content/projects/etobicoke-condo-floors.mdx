Condo flooring is its own discipline: building rules dictate acoustic underlayment specs, work hours, elevator booking, and material delivery procedures. Board approvals are not optional. The owners of this 1,200 sq.ft. Etobicoke unit had been quoted twice by retail flooring companies and turned down both times because they couldn't navigate the building's documentation requirements.

We worked with the condo property manager to submit the engineered-hardwood spec, acoustic underlayment certification (meeting the building's IIC 50/STC 50 requirement), and a contractor insurance binder. Approval came through in two weeks. We booked the freight elevator for the demolition and delivery days, set up dust containment at the unit's entry, and used HEPA vacuums throughout.

The original flooring was a mix of beige carpet (living, bedrooms, hallway) and outdated ceramic tile (entryway, kitchen). All of it came out. The subfloor needed levelling in three spots before the new floor went down. The replacement is a 7-inch wide-plank engineered hardwood with an oil finish — warm, matte, and slip-resistant compared to high-gloss alternatives.

Construction time: 8 days on site. Two days of subfloor prep, four days of installation, two days of finishing and reveals.
