This Leslieville detached home had a perfectly functional 1990s kitchen — laminate counters, oak cabinets, and a load-bearing wall between the kitchen and dining room. The owners had been living with it for eight years and were ready to commit to a full open-concept renovation.

The wall removal was the structural anchor of the project: an engineer-stamped flush beam transferred the second-floor load to new bearing points either side of the opening. With the wall gone, the kitchen became part of the main living space, and the island became the centerpiece.

Custom shaker cabinetry in a soft white paint, brass cup pulls, quartz countertops with a waterfall edge on the island, and a hand-laid subway tile backsplash. Wide-plank engineered hardwood runs continuously through the kitchen, dining, and living rooms — one of the simplest moves that makes the new space feel bigger.

The owners stayed in the home during the renovation, with a temporary kitchen set up in the basement. Total construction time: 11 weeks from demolition to final walkthrough.
