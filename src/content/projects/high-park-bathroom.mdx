This High Park primary bedroom en-suite was tired and tight: a tub-shower combo, single vanity, beige floor tile from the 1980s, and just enough room to turn around. The homeowners wanted a spa-grade bathroom with separate tub and shower — and they had the space if we re-worked the layout.

The key move was a curbless walk-in shower with a linear drain on the long wall. That alone bought enough room to fit a separate freestanding soaker tub against the window. Porcelain slab on the shower walls — book-matched so the veining flows continuously across both panels — replaces the look of dozens of grout joints with a single uninterrupted surface that's also faster to clean.

A custom double vanity with quartz top runs along the longest wall. Electric in-floor heat sits under the porcelain tile, controlled by a programmable thermostat. The shower has a thermostatic mixing valve, rainfall head, hand shower, and a body spray on a separate diverter.

Construction time: 5 weeks. The owners used the second-floor guest bathroom during the work; we phased the dust-barrier wall to protect the bedroom side of the home.
