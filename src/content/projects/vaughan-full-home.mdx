The owners bought this 3,200 sq.ft. detached Vaughan home with a plan: gut everything inside, open up the main floor, and bring the mechanicals up to current code. They hired an architect for the layout drawings and structural engineer for the beam calculations, then came to us to build it.

The scope reads like new construction without the foundation: every interior wall touched, full electrical rewire (and a 200A service upgrade), full plumbing repipe in PEX, three bathrooms reframed and refinished, a new kitchen, refinished original red oak floors on the main level, new flooring upstairs and downstairs, and a basement walk-out cut into the rear foundation wall.

The structural anchor of the project was a flush LVL beam replacing a load-bearing wall between the kitchen, living, and dining areas. Engineer-stamped, inspected, and signed off. The mechanicals were the bigger lift: the original wiring was 1980s aluminum in some circuits, the plumbing was galvanized in others. We pulled it all and re-ran everything to modern code while the walls were open.

The family moved out for the duration of construction (a 7-month rental). We held weekly site meetings with the owners and the architect, and shared progress photos and a running budget tracker by email. Total construction time: 7 months. Permits, design, and engineering took another 4 months on the front end.
