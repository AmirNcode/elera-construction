The honest answer to "how much does a kitchen renovation cost in the GTA?" is: it depends. But homeowners hate that answer, so here's a more useful version with real ranges, real drivers, and a way to figure out where your project will land before you talk to a contractor.

## Three tiers, real ranges

**Refresh / minor reno: $25,000–$45,000.**
You're keeping the existing kitchen layout. Doors are replaced (or cabinets are painted), countertops swapped (laminate or basic quartz), new backsplash, new sink and faucet, maybe new appliances. Floors stay. Layout stays. Mostly cosmetic.

**Mid-range gut: $50,000–$100,000.**
Full demo of the existing kitchen. New cabinetry (semi-custom), new quartz or mid-range stone countertops, new appliances (mid-range), new lighting, new flooring (if extending into the kitchen). Plumbing rough-ins may move within reason. No structural work.

**Premium / open-concept: $100,000–$250,000+.**
Full gut plus structural changes (load-bearing wall comes down, footprint expands), full custom cabinetry, premium stone countertops (porcelain slab, exotic granite), high-end integrated appliances (Sub-Zero, Wolf, Miele), in-floor heat, new HVAC routing, full electrical re-work for the kitchen zone.

## What drives the price up the most

In rough order of impact:

1. **Cabinetry tier.** Stock vs. semi-custom vs. full custom can swing $20–60K on the same kitchen footprint. Cabinetry is typically 40–55% of total project cost.
2. **Structural changes.** Removing a load-bearing wall adds $8–25K for the engineer, beam, and structural framing. Worth it if the layout improves significantly.
3. **Appliance package.** A standard $4K appliance package can easily become a $30K Sub-Zero + Wolf + Miele package. Personal preference, not necessarily worth it for everyone.
4. **Countertop material.** Standard quartz ($60–80/sq.ft. installed) vs. porcelain slab or exotic stone ($120–250/sq.ft.) on a 60 sq.ft. counter is $4–10K difference.
5. **Plumbing layout changes.** Moving the sink to an island, relocating the dishwasher, adding a pot filler — each move adds $500–2,000 in plumbing labour plus permit cost if required.

## What doesn't drive cost as much as people think

- **Backsplash tile.** Even premium tile is usually $1–3K total. The labour to install matters more than the material.
- **Paint colour.** Free.
- **Hardware (knobs, pulls).** $200–1,500 swing on most kitchens.
- **Lighting fixtures.** $1–4K total swing for most projects.

## How to scope your own project before getting quotes

1. **Take photos of your current kitchen** from four corners and the ceiling.
2. **Walk a comparable showroom or look at three real renovations** in your tier. Notice cabinetry door style, countertop edge profile, appliance brands.
3. **Make a list of must-have vs. nice-to-have changes.** Be specific: "double the counter run" not "more counter space."
4. **Set a top-end budget you'd hate to exceed.** That number tells a contractor whether your project is realistic before any drawings get made.
